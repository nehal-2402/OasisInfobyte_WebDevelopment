Become a Web Designer in 10 Days

Install tools that don’t require for you to code and that can work out of the box, for example you can install docker and download a docker recipe for wordpress or you can download a static file generator or a flat-file CMS that won’t require to use a database and should be easy to install. You will have a site up and running in a matter of a few hours, minutes if you are lucky. Choose a theme you like. Usually Wordpress and other CMS have different free themes or templates that are pre-built and you can re-use without any coding knowledge Fill the site with content. Upload your site to a host, there are a few free or cheap hostings out there that support many CMS or at least there is a trial where you can put your site live. Worst thing here is to figure out how to upload the files to the host, there are many different options, easiest is probably through FTP, you’ll need an FTP client for this. Launch your site and feel good about it (because you will feel bad when things are not getting the things as expected). Now you have completed the pre-requisites to start learning web development. Next, choose something you visually want to change or improve in your site and do a hell of research of how to do it. This is called front-end development.
